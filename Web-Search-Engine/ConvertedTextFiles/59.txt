The content Canadians need, the creators they love People all over Canada have used YouTube to follow their passions, connect with others, and develop economic opportunities for themselves and their communities. Meet the music artists, creative entrepreneurs, and small businesses owners across the country who call YouTube home. Every day, Canadians count on YouTube Whether it’s to build their future, learn new skills or inspire others. Deddy's Kitchen: Finding a new career When COVID happened, Brampton chef Deddy Ruddock & his daughter Sasha turned adversity into opportunity by starting a YouTube cooking show. With their fresh take and delicious Jamaican home cooking, they shared what they love and found a following - and a career. The Sorry Girls: Inspiring people to create Becky Wright and Kelsey MacDermaid started on YouTube in 2010, focused on small craft projects for a close knit creative community. Fast forward to today and their channel is now a creative outlet for full scale design projects that has resonated with do-ers across the globe. Faouzia: Sharing her music with the world Faouzia is a Morrocan-Canadian singer/songwriter who discovered music at a young age. At 15, she started to post videos of herself covering famous songs on her YouTube channel and her music resonated with people all over the world. Now with 1.6 million subscribers and tens of millions of views, she has landed a deal with a major music label. Jonathan Roy: Following his dreams When your father is a hockey Hall of Famer, it's hard to imagine a future as a musician. But, Jonathan Roy followed his dreams and through his YouTube channel, he's made a name as a songwriter. His YouTube featured single "Keeping Me Alive" has received international acclaim and over 50 million views, propelling him into the global limelight.
